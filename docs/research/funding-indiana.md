# Indiana State & Regional Funding Programs for LakeLife — Research Findings

Scope: state-level Indiana programs plus the northeast Indiana / LaGrange-Steuben regional layer. Verified via live web search and direct fetches on 2026-07-23. LakeLife is a founder-operated, pre-revenue, bootstrapped software marketplace forming an Indiana LLC — I've flagged where programs assume manufacturing/jobs/capital-investment profiles that don't match that shape.

---

## Tier 1 — Best realistic fit right now

### 1. Community Ideation Fund (via Elevate Northeast Indiana)
- **Administering body:** Elevate Ventures, deployed regionally through Elevate Northeast Indiana (Elevate NEI), funded by the state's 21st Century Research and Technology Fund.
- **What it gives:** $5,000–$20,000 investment for ideation-stage companies working toward a specific, measurable product/tech milestone.
- **Eligibility fit:** Strong. Must be HQ'd in one of the 11 counties Elevate NEI covers — **LaGrange and Steuben counties are both confirmed inside this footprint** (the 11 counties are Adams, Allen, DeKalb, Huntington, LaGrange, Kosciusko, Noble, Steuben, Wabash, Wells, Whitley). Trailing-12-month revenue must be ≤$50,000 — LakeLife's pre-revenue status qualifies cleanly. A precedent exists for exactly this kind of company: Elevate NEI funded "Slice," a cloud software startup for writers, through this fund.
- **Timing:** Rolling/no fixed deadline found; apply via the fund's page.
- **Effort:** Low-medium — single application, no pitch competition cycle required (per the fund's design).
- **Caveat:** This is a narrow window — LakeLife should apply *before* revenue climbs past $50K trailing, which given a "launching soon" timeline could happen fast.
- **URL:** https://elevatenei.com/community-ideation-fund/ (Note: this domain failed to resolve via my direct fetch tool twice — likely a network/DNS quirk on my end, not a dead site, since it's independently corroborated by Elevate Ventures' own blog posts and multiple news outlets. Verify by loading it in a normal browser before relying on it.)

### 2. Indiana Small Business Development Center (ISBDC) — Northeast Indiana office
- **Administering body:** ISBDC, hosted by IEDC.
- **What it gives:** No-cost, confidential one-on-one business advising, financial/loan-readiness prep, strategic planning. No direct dollars, but often the required first step before other IEDC-linked programs.
- **Eligibility fit:** Excellent — explicitly serves LaGrange and Steuben counties.
- **Timing:** Ongoing, appointment-based.
- **Effort:** Very low.
- **URL:** https://isbdc.org/locations/northeast-indiana-sbdc/

### 3. Elevate Ventures Pre-Seed Fund (statewide)
- **Administering body:** Elevate Ventures, in partnership with IEDC.
- **What it gives:** $20,000–$100,000 as a convertible note (20% discount, 8% interest, 3-year maturity).
- **Eligibility fit:** Moderate-good, but competitive. Requires Indiana commercial domicile, a **>$500M total addressable market** narrative, a committed team building a "venture-backed, high-growth" company, and willingness to pitch an investment committee. Software is an explicitly supported sector (real estate, insurance, and "SME/professional services" businesses are explicitly *excluded* per Elevate's own programs page — LakeLife should frame itself as a tech/software platform, not a "home services company," to stay on the right side of that line). $50K–$100K tier additionally wants a working MVP and customer-discovery evidence.
- **Timing:** Rolling, no competition cycle (replaced the old Elevate Nexus Pitch Competition).
- **Effort:** Medium-high — deck, 10-minute committee pitch, 2/3 committee vote, then Elevate + IEDC review, then 3 years of quarterly reporting post-investment.
- **URL:** https://elevateventures.com/resource/pre-seed-fund/

### 4. Venture Capital Investment (VCI) Tax Credit — for future investors, not LakeLife directly
- **Administering body:** IEDC.
- **What it gives:** A 25% (30% for W/MBE-owned) Indiana tax credit to *investors* who put capital into a certified "Qualified Indiana Business" (QIB), capped at $1M–$1.5M per investor lifetime; 20% credit for investments via a Qualified Investment Fund. Statewide annual cap is $20M.
- **Eligibility fit for LakeLife:** Good, and worth doing early. QIB certification requires: HQ in Indiana, average revenue <$10M/yr over the prior two years, and either being focused on tech commercialization/new technology application OR IEDC judging you to have "significant potential to bring substantial capital into Indiana, create jobs, [or] diversify the business base" — a broad, discretionary catch-all that a software marketplace can credibly argue. Also requires ≥50% of employees or ≥75% of assets in Indiana.
- **Important nuance to flag for counsel:** QIB certification and the credit apply to "qualified securities" (equity/debt investments) — most institutional and angel investors expect a Delaware or Indiana **C-corp** cap table, not an LLC membership interest, before this credit is cleanly usable. LakeLife should get IEDC guidance on whether/when to convert entity type if it wants angels to claim this credit.
- **Timing:** No deadline; certify as QIB via Access Indiana account before soliciting qualifying investment.
- **Effort:** Medium (paperwork, not a competitive pitch).
- **URL:** https://iedc.in.gov/indiana-advantages/investments/venture-capital-investment-tax-credit/overview

## Tier 2 — Real but county-level / mentorship-heavy, not big dollars

### 5. Trine University Innovation One (Angola, Steuben County)
- University-run incubator/accelerator directly in one of LakeLife's operating counties. Offers business-plan development, marketing help, and investor connections; historically hosted an Elevate Ventures entrepreneur-in-residence on campus. No confirmed direct dollar grant to companies (couldn't verify a specific funding line — flagging as unverified on the dollar amount).
- **Effort:** Low to engage, informal.
- **URL:** https://www.trine.edu/innovation-one/index.aspx

### 6. Steuben County Economic Development Corporation (SCEDC)
- **What it gives:** "Cowork Steuben" space ($50/month), a Revolving Loan Fund (amount/terms not published — requires a call), and "BELT" (Business and Entrepreneurship Launch Tool), a 10-week accelerator with mentorship and tools.
- **Eligibility fit:** Good as a local base of operations and networking; the revolving loan fund is likely structured for collateralized/job-creating loans rather than early equity-style funding, so treat it as a future working-capital tool, not seed capital.
- **Effort:** Low to start (coworking, BELT); loan fund needs direct inquiry since terms aren't public.
- **URL:** https://www.steubenedc.com/

### 7. LaGrange County Economic Development Corporation (LCEDC)
- **What it gives:** A $1.5M Revolving Loan Fund (loans capped at $200,000 or 10% of available fund balance, meant for expansions and "new entrepreneurial ventures," used alongside traditional financing — not a grant); a **LaGrange County Business Accelerator Program** (curriculum on strategic planning, business-model development, and investor-pitch prep); a Workplace Preparedness Grant (up to $1,000/worker for supplies — likely aimed at traditional W-2 employees, uncertain fit for LakeLife's 1099 vendor network, flag for direct confirmation); and a joint 10-week small-business cohort with Noble/Steuben/DeKalb counties (~$125 cost).
- **Eligibility fit:** Directly in LakeLife's home county (Big Long Lake near Wolcottville, Pretty Lake are both in LaGrange County). Good for the accelerator/education piece now; the loan fund is a later-stage tool once there's revenue and collateral to point to.
- **Effort:** Low (accelerator, cohort) to medium (loan fund application).
- **URL:** https://www.lagrangecountyedc.com/site-selection/our-business-incentives/

### 8. Northeast Indiana Innovation Center (NIIC), Fort Wayne
- Nonprofit tech incubator/accelerator (40,400 sq ft facility), Women's Business Center, coaching services; selected for a 2026 Rockefeller Foundation "Big Bets" fellowship (regional recognition, not a direct grant to individual startups). I could **not verify current 2026 pitch-competition specifics, costs, or whether non-Fort-Wayne/remote companies can formally join the incubator** — the site's public pages didn't surface program-level detail; recommend a direct call before investing effort.
- **Effort:** Unknown pending direct contact.
- **URL:** https://niic.net/

## Tier 3 — Exists, but poor fit or wrong stage for LakeLife right now

### 9. Innovation Voucher Program (via Applied Research Institute)
- Matching grant up to $50,000, but **requires partnering with a university or nonprofit research provider** on R&D (testing, product development). Could theoretically apply to LakeLife's "AI-driven dispatch/routing" R&D if partnered with a nearby university (Trine, Purdue Fort Wayne), but that's a real lift to set up and not a fit for a straightforward marketplace-software build. Administration transferred from Elevate Ventures to the Applied Research Institute (ARI) in late 2023; three 2026 application windows exist per search results, but I could not pull exact 2026 deadline dates from ARI's own portal.
- **Verdict:** Medium-stretch fit; only pursue if LakeLife wants to formalize the routing-algorithm work as a funded university research collaboration.
- **URL:** https://pathfinder.theari.us/innovationvoucher/home

### 10. FAST Program (Federal and State Technology Partnership)
- Matching grants and advising, but **exclusively for companies actively pursuing/holding federal SBIR/STTR awards**. LakeLife is a commercial marketplace product, not a federal-research-grant-track company.
- **Verdict:** Not a fit. Excluded from LakeLife's realistic pipeline.
- **URL:** https://iedc.in.gov/entrepreneurship/fast-program

### 11. Hoosier Business Investment (HBI) Tax Credit / EDGE Payroll Tax Credit
- Both are IEDC's flagship *large-project* incentives: HBI credits capital investment in machinery/buildings tied to net-new-job creation (up to 10–25% depending on category, capped $50M/yr statewide); EDGE credits a share of new payroll withholding tied to job creation, also requires "significant" matching incentives from the local municipality and that the credit be "a major factor" in the location decision. Neither publishes a minimum threshold, but both are structured around negotiated deals for employers adding meaningful headcount/capital investment — not a bootstrapped 1-3 person software startup.
- **Verdict:** Not realistic now. Revisit only if/when LakeLife opens a physical office or dispatch/ops hub in Indiana and is hiring a meaningful W-2 team — worth a call to IEDC's regional business development manager at that point.
- **URLs:** https://iedc.in.gov/indiana-advantages/investments/hoosier-business-investment-tax-credit/eligibility · https://iedc.in.gov/indiana-advantages/investments/economic-development-for-a-growing-economy-tax-credit/overview

### 12. Skills Enhancement Fund (SEF)
- Reimburses ~50% of employee training costs, but per IEDC's own program page it's tied to training required to support a **new capital investment project** — in practice it's typically bundled into larger HBI/EDGE deals, not a freestanding small-business training voucher (one secondary source called it "the most accessible" IEDC program for small business, up to $5,000/employee, but that conflicts with IEDC's own capital-investment framing — treat that claim as unverified).
- **Verdict:** Low priority now; revisit alongside HBI/EDGE at a later growth stage.
- **URL:** https://iedc.in.gov/indiana-advantages/investments/skills-enhancement-fund-sef/overview

### 13. Indiana 21st Century Research and Technology Fund ("21 Fund")
- Still exists — the 2016-era budget fight it survived is well documented, and it currently **funds Elevate Ventures' regional deployment vehicles** (including the Community Ideation Fund above) rather than accepting standalone applications from companies. **Don't look for a separate "21 Fund application"** — the way LakeLife accesses this money is through Elevate Ventures' Pre-Seed Fund and Community Ideation Fund (items #1 and #3 above).
- **Verdict:** Not a separate action item — already captured via #1/#3.

### 14. Indiana Community Collaboration Fund (CCF, formerly reported as part of a July 2026 IEDC announcement)
- $757,000 across 22 awards in the most recent round (July 2026), including a $35,000 award to Northeast Indiana Innovation Community and $40,000 to a youth-entrepreneurship program reaching NEI high schools. **Grantees are nonprofits, EDCs, universities, and chambers of commerce — not individual for-profit startups.** LakeLife itself cannot apply directly.
- **Verdict:** Not a direct funding source for LakeLife, but useful context: it's the money funding some of the mentorship/ecosystem programs above (NIIC, Elevate NEI), and signals the state is actively investing in this exact region's entrepreneurship pipeline right now.
- **URL:** https://iedc.in.gov/events/news/details/2026/07/06/gov.-braun-strengthens-indiana-entrepreneurship-through-community-grants

### 15. SSBCI Capital Access Program / Legend Fund
- Federally-seeded ($99M+ to Indiana) credit-enhancement and loan-participation programs that work through **participating local lenders** (the $29M "Legend Fund" purchases portions of loans made by mission-driven lenders to small businesses, with priority for underserved owners). Not a grant to LakeLife directly — it's a mechanism that could make a future bank/credit-union working-capital loan (e.g., to float the vendor ACH early-pay program) more available/affordable once LakeLife has revenue history.
- **Verdict:** Bookmark for later-stage debt financing; ask a local lender (e.g., a northeast-Indiana community bank or credit union) whether they participate in CAP-SSBCI or the Legend Fund.
- **URL:** https://iedc.in.gov/ssbci

## Explicitly flagged as not a fit / out of scope
- **OCRA (Office of Community and Rural Affairs) rural grants** — LaGrange and Steuben are rural, OCRA-eligible counties, but OCRA grants go to units of local government and nonprofits, not private LLCs. Not directly applicable to LakeLife.
- **FAST Program** — SBIR/STTR-only, excluded (see #10).
- **HBI / EDGE / SEF** — sized and structured for job-creating capital projects, not a pre-revenue software startup at this stage (see #11–12).

---

## Bottom line — recommended near-term action order
1. Call the Northeast Indiana ISBDC office now — free, low-effort, and they can help package everything below.
2. Apply to the Community Ideation Fund via Elevate NEI before trailing revenue exceeds $50K.
3. Get QIB-certified with IEDC (VCI tax credit) in parallel — costs nothing and makes any future angel money more attractive, but loop in counsel on the LLC-vs-C-corp question first.
4. In parallel, engage LaGrange County EDC's Business Accelerator and/or Steuben County's BELT program and Trine's Innovation One — cheap, local, and useful pitch-prep before approaching Elevate's Pre-Seed Fund committee.
5. Target the Elevate Ventures Pre-Seed Fund ($20K–$100K) once there's a working MVP and a credible >$500M TAM story — this is the biggest realistic near-term check, but the most competitive.

Everything above was checked against live sources on 2026-07-23; where I couldn't independently verify a claim (Elevate NEI's own site content, exact ARI voucher deadlines, NIIC's current pitch-competition mechanics, SEF's "$5,000/employee, most accessible" claim), I've flagged it explicitly rather than presenting it as confirmed.
