EXECUTIVE REVIEW MEMORANDUM
Purpose. This package merges the production-system mechanics in LakeLife Draft v1 with stronger marketplace, payment, indemnity, storage, insurance, consumer, and dispute provisions. It is designed as a counsel-ready working draft, not a substitute for licensed legal review in each operating jurisdiction.
Priority issue
Review direction
Payment flow is the highest-risk issue
The agent-of-payee language is substantially strengthened, but the actual card processor -> LakeLife settlement account -> ACH flow must be reviewed against the processor contract, federal payment-processor guidance, and every state in which LakeLife operates. Contract language alone cannot cure a regulated funds-flow structure.
Customer discharge is now explicit
The revised Section 7 states when discharge occurs, bars duplicate collection, assigns the Crew's recourse to LakeLife, and carves out dishonor, reversal, fraud, chargeback, and refund.
Storage is allocated directly to the Crew
The revised storage section creates the bailment between Customer and Crew, disclaims LakeLife possession, requires custody coverage, and adds a conservative contractual lien/unclaimed-property procedure that expressly defers to mandatory vessel, trailer, title, lien, notice, and disposal law.
Independent-contractor facts are preserved
The revised terms preserve Crew rate-setting, acceptance rights, equipment, staffing, scheduling, multiple trucks, outside work, and absence of guaranteed volume while separating neutral Platform standards from control over manner and means.
Insurance minimums
The draft inserts $1M/$2M CGL, $1M auto, statutory workers' compensation, and $500K employer's liability. For storage, the better rule is coverage at least equal to the maximum aggregate customer-property value in the Crew's custody, not a flat low limit.
Consumer booking overlays
The agreement now authorizes Service-specific statutory disclosures and separate contracts. Counsel should map which offerings may be home improvements, marine repair/storage, towing, pesticide application, or other regulated services in each operating state.
Clickwrap implementation
Store the accepted version ID, full rendered text or immutable hash, timestamp, account ID, IP/device evidence, checkbox state, and the precise Service Order disclosures. Place arbitration/class waiver and automatic-charge disclosure immediately above the acceptance control.

1. Agreement; Acceptance; Electronic Contracting
This LakeLife User Agreement, together with the Privacy Policy, the then-current booking details, cancellation disclosures, Service-specific disclosures, and policies expressly incorporated by reference (collectively, the "Agreement"), is a legally binding agreement among LakeLife, LLC, an Indiana limited liability company ("LakeLife," "we," "us," or "our"), each Customer, and each Crew, as applicable to their respective roles. A "Customer" is a person or entity requesting or purchasing Services. A "Crew" is an independently owned service business offering or performing Services through the Platform. Each is a "User."
A Customer accepts this Agreement by affirmatively checking the acceptance box and tapping "Agree" before the Customer's first booking. A Crew accepts it before its profile may go live or receive work. The person accepting for an entity represents that the person has authority to bind that entity. Electronic acceptance, account authentication, booking confirmation, job claim, completion submission, and payment authorization have the same force as a handwritten signature. LakeLife will require renewed affirmative acceptance before a User's next booking or job when a material amendment takes effect. Mere browsing does not constitute acceptance. No User may use the Platform without accepting the then-current Agreement.
2. Definitions and Rules of Interpretation
"All-In Price" means the total Customer-facing price displayed and accepted at booking for the stated scope, excluding only separately disclosed and later accepted change orders, cancellation charges, post-season storage charges, taxes required to be separately stated, and other charges expressly authorized by the Customer. "Crew Proceeds" means the take-home amount displayed to and accepted by the Crew, less authorized offsets, reversals, refunds, chargebacks, reserves, taxes, and fees. "Payment Processor" means LakeLife's third-party card, ACH, banking, or payout provider. "Platform" means LakeLife's website, applications, booking, matching, dispatch, routing, photo-verification, messaging, records, pricing, and payment-administration systems. "Services" means lake-home and related services offered by independent Crews, including lawn care, housekeeping, pier and lift work, winterization, hauling, and boat storage. "Service Agreement" means the direct contract between a Customer and Crew formed under Section 4. "Service Order" means the booking record, accepted scope, property profile, price, timing, disclosures, accepted offers, and written change orders for a particular job.
"Including" means "including without limitation." Section references are to this Agreement. The singular includes the plural. If a Service Order conflicts with this Agreement, the Service Order controls only as to the commercial scope, timing, and price of that job; this Agreement controls as to LakeLife's role, payments, risk allocation, indemnity, liability limitations, and dispute resolution.
3. LakeLife's Role; Third-Party Administrator; No Service Provider Relationship
LakeLife provides technology and administrative infrastructure that enables Customers and independent Crews to find one another, contract, schedule, route, communicate, document completion, and facilitate payment. LakeLife does not itself perform, furnish, supervise, inspect, warrant, or control any Service. LakeLife is not a contractor, subcontractor, employer, joint employer, staffing company, partner, joint venturer, franchise, insurer, guarantor, warehouseman, bailee, broker, fiduciary, or general agent of any User. LakeLife is not a party to a Service Agreement except solely in its expressly limited capacity as a Crew's payment-collection agent under Section 7.
LakeLife may establish neutral Platform eligibility rules, document requirements, completion-photo requirements, customer-service processes, safety-related access rules, and standards for continued Platform access. Those Platform rules protect the marketplace and do not give LakeLife the right or responsibility to control the manner, means, sequence, tools, staffing, judgment, or physical performance of a Crew's work. Matching, routing, ratings, document review, photos, customer support, or payment administration do not constitute supervision, inspection, certification, endorsement, or acceptance of a Crew or its work.
4. Direct Service Agreement Between Customer and Crew
A Service Agreement forms directly and exclusively between the Customer and the Crew when the Platform identifies the Crew for a booked Service and the Crew accepts, claims, or begins that Service. The Service Agreement consists of this Agreement as standing terms between Customer and Crew, the applicable Service Order, and any written change order accepted through the Platform. LakeLife is an intended third-party beneficiary of all provisions protecting LakeLife.
The Crew, not LakeLife, is the contracting service provider and is solely responsible for performance, personnel, means and methods, safety, licensing, permits, materials, equipment, legal compliance, warranties, correction of defective work, and damage caused by the Crew. The Customer is solely responsible for accurate property information, authority to order the work, safe lawful access, disclosed hazards, and payment. Any claim based on the design, performance, nonperformance, delay, quality, safety, warranty, storage, custody, or results of a Service lies between Customer and Crew, subject to LakeLife's separate payment-administration rights.
No oral statement by LakeLife changes a Service Order or creates a warranty. A Crew may not bind LakeLife, and a Customer may not rely on a Crew as speaking for LakeLife.

6. Booking, Scope, Pricing, Changes, and Customer Payment Authorization
6.1 Service Order and All-In Price. The Platform calculates the All-In Price from the published menu, property profile, selected scope, timing, and accepted options displayed at booking. The Customer must review the Service Order before confirmation. The All-In Price covers only the stated scope and disclosed assumptions.
6.2 Card on File and Automatic Charge. A valid payment method is required. By booking, the Customer irrevocably authorizes LakeLife and the Payment Processor to store a tokenized credential, verify the payment method, and charge it when the Crew marks the job complete and submits the required photographs, subject to LakeLife's administrative review. The authorization also covers accepted change orders, accepted scarcity or rush offers, cancellation fees, post-season storage charges, refunds or correction entries, taxes, collection costs where lawful, and other amounts expressly authorized under the Service Order or Agreement. LakeLife does not store full card numbers.
6.3 Completion Is Not a Warranty. Photo submission and Platform status are administrative payment triggers, not an inspection, warranty, or determination that work is defect-free. A Customer must promptly report an objectively documented issue through the Platform. LakeLife may temporarily hold or adjust payment while facilitating communications but is not required to adjudicate the underlying Service dispute.
6.4 Price Offers and Change Orders. Scarcity offers, same-day rush premiums, and other price changes are never effective unless affirmatively accepted in the Platform. A Crew may not demand an off-Platform surcharge. Work materially outside the booked scope requires a written Platform change order accepted by the Customer, except emergency work reasonably necessary to prevent imminent personal injury or material property damage.
6.5 Autopilot. Autopilot is a scheduling aid, not an automatic purchase. The price is locked as displayed at enrollment, but each proposed visit must be affirmatively confirmed before booking. The Customer may disable Autopilot prospectively at any time.
6.6 Credits. Referral and promotional credits are limited, revocable rebates for future Platform purchases. They are not stored value, deposits, property held for the User, gift cards, or cash equivalents; have no cash value; are nontransferable; may expire as disclosed; and may be reversed for fraud, refund, chargeback, or program abuse.
7. Payment Services; Limited Payment Agency; Discharge of Customer Obligation
7.1 Processor and Restricted Purpose. Payment services are provided through one or more Payment Processors. LakeLife is not a bank, escrow company, trustee, money depository, or provider of general-purpose funds transmission. LakeLife accepts and transmits funds only as an integral part of settling bona fide Service Agreements on the Platform and will not accept funds for unrelated transfers, person-to-person payments, cash equivalents, or sham transactions. Users must comply with applicable Payment Processor terms.
7.2 Crew Appointment. Each Crew irrevocably appoints LakeLife as the Crew's disclosed, limited payment-collection agent solely to: (a) collect amounts owed by Customers under Service Agreements; (b) direct the Payment Processor regarding authorization, capture, allocation, refund, reversal, reserve, offset, and payout; (c) deduct LakeLife fees and other authorized amounts; and (d) remit net Crew Proceeds to the Crew's designated account. This appointment does not authorize LakeLife to perform Services, direct work, bind the Crew to a job without acceptance, make warranties for the Crew, or act as the Crew's general agent.
7.3 Full Discharge Upon Successful Payment. To the maximum extent permitted by law, when a Customer's authorized payment is successfully received by LakeLife or the Payment Processor, the Customer's payment obligation to the Crew is fully and finally discharged in the amount received exactly as though the Customer paid the Crew directly. The Crew shall not seek duplicate payment from the Customer. The Crew's sole recourse for LakeLife's subsequent failure to remit collected funds is against LakeLife, not the Customer, except to the extent the Customer payment is later dishonored, reversed, charged back, determined fraudulent, or refunded.
7.4 No Escrow; No Beneficial Interest. Unless a separate written agreement expressly states otherwise, collected funds are not held in escrow or trust, do not earn interest for Users, and may be commingled or maintained through processor-controlled settlement accounts as permitted by applicable law and processor rules. A hold, reserve, delayed payout, or milestone is an administrative risk-control feature, not an escrow service.
7.5 Payouts. For completed, photo-submitted jobs, LakeLife releases Crew Proceeds after Customer billing and risk review. Standard monthly payout is without a LakeLife payout fee. An earlier payout may be offered for the disclosed fee then shown in the Crew app. Payout dates are estimates and remain subject to processor settlement, weekends, bank delays, identity and tax verification, fraud review, disputes, chargebacks, reserves, legal process, and accurate bank instructions.
7.6 Holds, Reserves, Refunds, Offsets, and Negative Balances. LakeLife may delay or withhold payout, establish a reasonable reserve, issue a refund or credit, reverse a payout, or offset current or future Crew Proceeds when reasonably related to a cancellation, duplicate or erroneous charge, Customer dispute, documented nonperformance, fraud, chargeback, refund, safety incident, storage claim, tax withholding, processor requirement, legal obligation, breach, or amount owed by the Crew. The Crew authorizes recovery by deduction from future payouts, reserve, authorized bank debit where separately authorized, card charge, invoice, or lawful collection. Crew obligations for chargebacks and negative balances survive termination.
7.7 No Credit. LakeLife does not extend credit or guarantee Customer payment. Any accelerated payout before final settlement is provisional and may be reversed.
7.8 Payment-Flow Compliance Condition. LakeLife may alter settlement mechanics, processor, account structure, payout timing, or contractual terminology as reasonably necessary to comply with financial-services law or processor requirements, provided the Customer's discharge protection in Section 7.3 is preserved to the maximum extent legally permissible.

10. Boat Storage, Winterization, Bailment, Liens, and Unclaimed Property
10.1 Direct Bailment. A storage Service creates a bailment directly between the Customer, as bailor, and the storing Crew, as bailee. Possession, care, custody, and control transfer to the Crew upon physical receipt and remain with the Crew until physical return to the Customer or authorized recipient. LakeLife never takes possession and is not a bailee, warehouseman, marina, storage operator, or insurer.
10.2 Crew Duties. The Crew must use commercially reasonable care, comply with the Service Order, maintain the required garagekeepers or bailee coverage, secure the storage location, document intake and return condition, and promptly report loss, theft, damage, seizure, or material deterioration. The Crew remains responsible for its employees, subcontractors, storage site, transport, and custody. No Platform photograph waives a latent claim or establishes causation.
10.3 Customer Warranties. The Customer warrants ownership or authority to store the boat and associated property; must disclose liens, hazardous materials, fuel conditions, defects, alarms, keys, trailers, accessories, and special handling requirements; and must maintain hull and property coverage unless expressly agreed otherwise. Personal property should be removed unless itemized in the Service Order.
10.4 Season and Holdover. The storage season and daily holdover charge are the dates and rate displayed at booking. After the season end, the agreed per-diem accrues until the boat is collected, returned, splashed, lawfully sold, or otherwise disposed of. The Customer authorizes recurring charges for accrued holdover amounts.
10.5 Crew Lien and Enforcement. To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Customer grants the storing Crew a contractual possessory lien on the stored boat, trailer, equipment, and accessories for unpaid storage, transport, winterization, repair, preservation, towing, sale, notice, and enforcement charges. The Crew may retain possession while undisputed charges remain unpaid. Any foreclosure, sale, title process, disposal, or treatment as abandoned property must strictly comply with applicable lien, notice, title, consumer, and unclaimed-property law. LakeLife may provide records and payment administration but will not conduct a lien sale or take title unless separately authorized in a compliant written instrument.
10.6 Unclaimed Property Procedure. Before treating stored property as unclaimed, the Crew must use commercially reasonable efforts and all notices required by law to contact the Customer and known lienholders at available addresses, provide an itemized balance and retrieval deadline, and obtain legal guidance appropriate to the vessel, trailer, title status, and storage jurisdiction. No clause in this Agreement authorizes self-help inconsistent with mandatory law.
